Veviad gives one reliable place to answer: **what do we own, where is it, who is responsible, what condition is it in, and what must happen next?**

## Core concepts

| Concept | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Asset** | A uniquely tracked physical item with lifecycle, assignment, finance, maintenance, and audit history |
| **Person** | An assignable workforce record; it does not automatically grant application access |
| **Application user** | A person who can sign in and receives permissions through a role |
| **Checklist** | An asset-only procedure captured from a versioned template snapshot |
| **Action** | Event-driven or scheduled automation that creates work or notifications |
| **Report** | A reusable dataset definition with fields, filters, charts, schedules, runs, and optional AI summary |

## Recommended path

1. Read [Access and navigation](/getting-started/access-and-navigation).
2. Complete [Your first workspace](/getting-started/your-first-workspace).
3. Set [Profile and preferences](/getting-started/profile-and-preferences).
4. Learn [Daily work](/main).
5. Use [Administration](/organization) only for areas you manage.

## Permission-dependent behavior

Veviad hides or disables actions that your role cannot use. A missing module is normally an authorization decision, not missing data. Ask an administrator which responsibility your role is intended to cover.
