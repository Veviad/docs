Asset configuration controls the choices, rules, and additional data shown on asset records.

## Before you begin

You need the relevant asset Settings permission. Plan category and custom-field changes before applying them to a large inventory.

## Configure assets

1. Open **Settings** and choose an asset configuration area.
2. Maintain categories and their depreciation rules.
3. Configure status labels, including assignment requirements.
4. Configure asset tag generation and printable labels.
5. Build custom-field sets, define validation, and assign sets to categories.
6. Review field visibility and required-field settings.

## Expected result

Asset forms and the lazy-loaded asset grid reflect the new configuration without changing unrelated records.

## Best practices

- Use categories for stable reporting dimensions.
- Use custom fields for industry-specific data such as operating hours or service dates.
- Test required fields against imports and PDF intake before rollout.

## Troubleshooting

If a custom field is absent, verify that its field set is active and assigned to the asset category.
