## Purpose

SSO authenticates users. SCIM provisions identities. Veviad supports two separate SCIM purposes so workforce records do not automatically become application users.

![SSO and SCIM settings](/images/screenshots/scim-access.webp)

## Before you begin

You need identity integration permission and an administrator in the identity provider. Decide which system is authoritative for profiles, access, credentials, and roles.

## Configure SSO

1. Open **Admin → Integrations**, then open the **Identity Provider** integration.
2. Select **Azure Entra ID**, **Generic OIDC**, or **Generic SAML2**.
3. Enter the required provider values on the **SSO** tab.
4. Open **Role Mapping** to map IdP groups or SAML attributes to Veviad roles and choose the safe default role.
5. When enabling a new or changed connection, select **Test connection** before saving.
6. Select **Save configuration** from either tab. Veviad saves the SSO settings, activation state, mappings for the selected provider, and default role together.
7. Verify sign-in with a non-admin test account.

### Provider changes and activation

Role mappings are provider-specific. In the form, switching providers loads that provider's mappings and resets the activation switch. Complete and save the selected provider before enabling it.

A complete saved configuration can be activated or deactivated from the integrations table. Reactivating an unchanged saved configuration does not require another connection test. After connection-critical provider fields change, test the connection successfully before saving SSO as enabled. If table activation finds incomplete settings, Veviad leaves SSO inactive and opens the configuration dialog.

![SSO role mappings](/images/screenshots/sso-role-mapping.webp)

## Configure SCIM by purpose

### Application access

Use the application-access endpoint and token for people who should sign in. Provisioning controls access lifecycle and can synchronize role mappings.

### People directory

Use the workforce-directory endpoint and token for assignable people who may never sign in. This feed maintains profile and organization data without granting application access.

![People-directory SCIM configuration](/images/screenshots/scim-people-directory.webp)

## Expected result

SSO handles authentication, application SCIM controls login entitlement, and People SCIM maintains assignment-ready workforce records.

## Best practices

- Use separate enterprise applications, tokens, and groups for the two SCIM purposes.
- Regenerating a token invalidates the previous token.
- Pilot create, update, deactivate, and reactivation.
- Keep one authority per field.

## Troubleshooting

A synchronized person who cannot sign in may be correctly provisioned only to the People directory. Check purpose, access state, role mapping, and credential authority.
