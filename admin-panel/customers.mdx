Customer records connect a company and contact to one or more application instances. Open **Customers** from the Management section.

## Required and optional data

| Field | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Company Name | Required display name for the organization. |
| Email | Required contact address. It is also relevant to initial-admin creation and automatic Trial identity matching. |
| Name, Phone | Optional primary contact details. |
| Status | `active`, `inactive`, or `archived`. Prefer archiving over deletion when history must remain. |
| Notes | Internal operating context; do not store passwords or API keys. |
| Upgrade email preference | Per-customer override for successful instance-upgrade notifications. `Default` inherits the global setting; the global setting remains the master switch and a customer override cannot re-enable globally disabled mail. |

Only `active` customers are offered in the new-instance form. Use `inactive` or `archived` only when no new instance should be assigned.

## Create a customer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check for an existing record">
    Search the customer list by company name, then verify the email on possible matches. Reusing the correct record avoids split instance history and ambiguous Trial matching.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open New Customer">
    Enter the company name and a valid email. Add the contact name, phone, status, and operational notes when known.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and verify">
    Open the customer detail page and confirm the displayed data. Related instances appear on the right after they have been assigned.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Multiple customer records with the same normalized email can block automatic Trial provisioning because Central cannot choose an unambiguous owner. Resolve duplicates before retrying the Trial.
</Warning>

## Edit and archive

Use the customer detail page to update contact data or the upgrade-email preference. Changing the email can affect future initial-user communication and Trial-to-customer matching; verify the new address before saving.

Set a customer to `archived` when the relationship is no longer active but records must remain. Delete only after confirming that no instance or other relevant relation still uses the customer. Some delete buttons use a simple browser confirmation and deletion may fail when Directus relations protect the record.

## Success checks

- The customer appears once in the list with the intended status.
- The detail page shows the correct contact and upgrade-email preference.
- Every related instance belongs to the expected customer.
- No secrets or credentials are stored in Notes.

## Escalate when

- duplicate emails cannot be safely consolidated;
- an expected related instance is missing or belongs to another customer;
- a delete operation is blocked by an unknown relation;
- the contact change could redirect credentials or operational email to the wrong person.
