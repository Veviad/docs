## Purpose

Maintenance records capture planned and completed work, responsible suppliers, dates, costs, notes, documents, and the originating schedule or Action.

![Machinery maintenance history on an asset](/images/screenshots/maintenance.webp)

## Before you begin

You need Asset view and maintenance permission. The asset and any supplier or maintenance type must already exist.

## Record maintenance

1. Open the asset and its **Maintenance** section.
2. Create a maintenance record.
3. Choose the maintenance type, supplier, start date, expected scope, and notes.
4. Add cost and supporting documents when known.
5. Mark the record complete and record the completion date.
6. Review the next service date or schedule.

## Expected result

The maintenance remains part of the asset lifecycle and is available to Reports, audit history, and automation.

## Best practices

- Separate preventive, inspection, calibration, and repair work.
- Use attachments for evidence; keep secrets and personal data out of filenames.
- Complete records promptly so dashboard and reports remain accurate.
- Use scheduled Actions for recurring work at scale.

## Troubleshooting

If a maintenance record cannot be created, check the asset permission and required type. If scheduled work is missing, review the Action, schedule timezone, and queue health.
